Bubble Wrap Making Machine

  • This machine creates bubble wrap from a single roll of double-layer plastic, filling air in equidistant spots, and sealing the wrap with heat to form another roll of bubble bubble wrap. The machine uses two rollers, one where it pulls the double-layer plastic from, and the other where the final product of bubble wrap is stored. The plastic wrap goes through a few rollers, where first air is injected by one of 13 air tubes, and then immediately sealed by another roller that is heated. The rollers are just cylinders that are anchored on one side. It operates by itself until the source plastic is emptied. The machine itself is not in a protected enclosure, and so the plastic is just slotted in, like magnetic tape in a player, or like the tape in a cassette.
    • Source: "This Bubble Wrap Machine" by The_Tech_Monkey on reddit
    • Reflection: My main strategy in the above explanation is to work through the steps of the process, as well as increase the depth of the explanations in each new sentence. I also used imagery and analogy to describe the machine. Rereading it myself, outloud, I think it makes sense.

Portable High-rise Apartment Moving Elevator Vehicle

  • Imagine if you had to move into a high-rise apartment -- that does not have an elevator -- in a narrow street, how would you go about it? Your best bet is to raise your heavy furniture or other goods with a Portable Vehicle Delivery Elevator. It is hitched and towed with your moving truck. When you arrive at your destination, strong stabilizers are extended along each side of the elevator, and make contact with the road at an acute angle, which helps keep the elevator stable. Then, like a fireman’s ladder, the backbone of the elevator extends to the window of your apartment. Then, a flat, seat-like car goes up the backbone to your apartment window, where your furniture and goods can be easily moved in. Then, the car goes back down the backbone to pick up more goods. The reverse of this process happens for if or when you move out.
    • Source: "Machine for helping people move in small towns" by fibeep on reddit
    • Reflection: My approach here for this second specialized tool was to explain it like I would to a friend. Hooking them in with why you would need it. Then some technical description followed by analogies of backbone and firemen’s ladder. I think the more informal explanation here helps.
